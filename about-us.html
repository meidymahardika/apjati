About Us

We provide Amazing Solutions

Association of Indonesian Manpower Service Companies is a leading professional organization dedicated to the development and management of Indonesia’s international workforce. Established in 1982, Association of Indonesian Manpower Service Companies has been at the forefront of connecting Indonesian talent with global opportunities, ensuring the highest standards of professionalism, ethics, and worker welfare.

With a steadfast commitment to advancing manpower placement sector, Association of Indonesian Manpower Service Companies serves as a trusted partner to both local recruitment agencies and international employers. Our mission is to empower Indonesian workers by equipping them with the skills and knowledge needed to excel in global markets, while ensuring their rights and dignity are upheld at every stage of their professional journey.

Over the years, Association of Indonesian Manpower Service Companies has built a reputation for excellence by fostering collaboration with governments, private organizations, and international stakeholders. By advocating for fair and ethical practices, we aim to enhance the global competitiveness of Indonesia's workforce and contribute to the nation’s economic growth.

As the voice of manpower placement sector, Association of Indonesian Manpower Service Companies plays a vital role in policy development, workforce training, and the promotion of international partnerships. Our comprehensive approach ensures that every worker we support is prepared, protected, and positioned for success in their overseas endeavors.

Through our unwavering dedication, Association of Indonesian Manpower Service Companies continues to strengthen Indonesia’s presence on the global stage, creating opportunities that not only transform individual lives but also drive progress for communities and the nation as a whole.

1982
Indonesia Manpower Services Association (IMSA) was initially established as a unifying platform for Indonesian Migrant Worker Placement Companies.
1994
Ministry of Manpower restructured all Business Licenses for Placement Services (SIUP) for Private Employment Agencies in Indonesia, which indirectly created a stagnant situation for IMSA members, causing them to cease functioning as they had previously.
1995
Recognizing the importance of private employment agencies as a professional community and a platform for Indonesian manpower service entrepreneurs, the leaders of Indonesian Private Migrant Worker Placement Agencies re-established the Indonesian Manpower Services Association (known in Indonesia as APJATI) in Jakarta on May 29, 1995, for an indefinite period.
