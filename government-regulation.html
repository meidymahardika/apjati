Government Regulation

Understand the policies and regulations that guide our operations and ensure compliance

Indonesia has implemented a robust regulatory framework to govern migrant workers, addressing their placement, protection, and rights at every stage of their journey. Below is a comprehensive breakdown of the key laws, policies, and enforcement mechanisms

LEGAL FRAMEWORK

Law No. 18 of 2017 on the Protection of Indonesian Migrant Workers (known in Indonesia as PMI)

This is the cornerstone of Indonesia’s migrant worker regulations, replacing the outdated Law No. 39 of 2004.

It emphasizes:

• Worker Protection: Ensuring migrant workers’ rights are upheld domestically and abroad

• Human Trafficking Prevention: Measures to combat illegal recruitment and trafficking

• Integrated Support: Combining local, national, and international resources to safeguard workers

Key components:

• Placement and Licensing

Migrant workers must be placed only by authorized private agencies or government institutions

• Pre-Departure Orientation

Workers must undergo training, including cultural orientation and financial literacy

• Employment Contracts

Detailed agreements must be in place, specifying work conditions, wages, and legal protections

• Health and Social Security

Access to insurance (health, life, and work accident) and participation in social security programs

IMPLEMENTING REGULATIONS

Government Regulation No. 59 of 2021

• Implements the provisions of Law No. 18/2017

• Strengthens:

○ Licensing criteria for private placement agencies

○ Mechanisms for dispute resolution

○ Monitoring of migrant worker conditions abroad

○ Provisions for shelters and repatriation for workers in distress

Ministerial Regulations

• Minister of Manpower Regulation No. 10 of 2019: Technical guidelines for the placement and protection of migrant workers

• Minister of Foreign Affairs Regulation No. 8 of 2019: Outlines the roles of Indonesian embassies in worker protection

• Minister of Health Regulations: Set health requirements and ensure medical screenings for outbound workers
